# Voice

Things We Measure does not speak as an authority looking down on a subject.

It speaks as a careful observer standing within a complex system.

The writing should invite attention rather than demand agreement.

It should expand understanding rather than simplify complexity.

The goal is not to persuade.

The goal is to help people see more carefully.

## Characteristics
The writing should be:

- Observational
- Human
- Precise
- Restrained
- Curious
- Honest about uncertainty
- Grounded in evidence
- Respectful of lived experience

## We Prefer
We prefer observations over conclusions.

We prefer questions over declarations.

We prefer specificity over abstraction.

We prefer clarity over cleverness.

We prefer restraint over drama.

We prefer complexity over false certainty.

We prefer understanding over persuasion.

## Sentences We Admire
"Housing placement is often the beginning of recovery rather than the end of instability."

"Prevention success often appears as the absence of crisis."

"Policy narratives often move faster than lived reality."

"Success does not erase what came before."

"The reporting period ends. Recovery does not."
